Interrogative Patterns

Direct Question

[Question Word] + は?

Place the question word at the beginning.

"あなたの なまえは なんですか?"

Question + Context

[Sentence] + ですか?

Add です か to the end of a statement to make it a question.

"まいにち こーひーを のみますか?"

Important Note:

The particle は (wa) is used for topics/questions, but when asking "what" specifically about the object, use を (wo) instead.

Existence & Possession (ある・いる)

Things (Objects)

あります

For inanimate objects (things, items)

ねこがあります。 There is a cat.
ほんがあります。 There are books.

People & Animals

います

For living things (people, animals)

がくせいがいます。 There is a student.
いぬがいます。 There is a dog.

Key Difference

あります (arimasu) = for things (non-living)
います (imasu) = for people and animals (living things)

Incorrect: がくせいがあります ➔ Correct: がくせいがいます

Must / Obligation (～なければならない)

Expresses obligation or necessity.

～なければならない Must Do

れい: べんきょうしなければなりません

"I must study for the exam."

～てはいけない Must Not

れい: ここで きつえんしてはいけません

"You must not smoke here."

Should / Suggestion (～ほうがいい)

Used for advice or suggestions.

～ほうがいい You should...
はやく ねたほうがいい
～たらどう? Why don't you...?
こーひーを のんだらどう?
～ようがいい It would be better to...
いしゃに いったようがいい
～べき Should (stronger)
しんじつを いうべき

Verb Tense Reference (Formal ~masu)

Form Ending Example
Present (+) ~ます たべます (Eat)
Present (-) ~ません たべません (Don't eat)
Past (+) ~ました たべました (Ate)
Past (-) ~ませんでした たべませんでした (Didn't eat)

* Note: These endings apply to all Verb Groups in their formal polite form.

Identification vs Description

Adj + Noun:
Takai yama (High mountain)

Subject + ga + Adj:
Yama ga takai desu (The mountain is high)

* Remember: NA-adj needs "na" to connect: Hima na hito

Connectors (But...)

To contrast ideas use the particle ~ga after the first adjective or verb:

[Sentence 1] + ga, [Sentence 2].

"Kono kasa wa benri desu ga, takai desu."
(This umbrella is convenient, but it is expensive).
